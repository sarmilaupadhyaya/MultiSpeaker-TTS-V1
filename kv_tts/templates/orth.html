Louisiana Creole Orthography Converter and Normalizer

Hi this is a combined orthographic converter and text normalizer for Louisiana Creole. This tool uses Epitran and purpose built rulesets for grapheme-phoneme-grapheme conversions
I wrote this program with the triple goal of making older texts easier to read, making it easier to make language models, and gaining experience in language technology.
Try it out on some folktales
This converter is a work in progress, and was created to solve some of the issues noted here by Oliver Mayeux: Louisiana Creole Orthography Tool

Namely, most of the issues with intervocalic nasals have been resolved, and transliteration from French spelling to the Kouri-Vini orthography takes etymology into consideration.
Two remaining challenges are incorporating etymology when transliterating IPA/DLC and word sense disambiguation for adding circumflexes.
There is some support for capitalization as well.
Adjust the window size by clicking and dragging as needed.

Spelling options (Most to least etymological)


French: Mainly used for historic texts through the 20th century. Note: This program converts spellings; it is not a French to Louisiana Creole translator.
Kouri Vini: Standard developed by Christophe Landry (Guide to Louisiana Creole Orthography) and used in the Ti Liv Kréyòl
DLC: The system developed by Thomas A. Klingler and used in the Dictionary of Louisiana Creole.
IPA: The International Phonetic Alphabet. Note: Since the focus is spelling, some distinctions made by linguists are collapsed here.
