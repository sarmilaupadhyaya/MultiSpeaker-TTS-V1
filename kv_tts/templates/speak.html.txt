<h1>Louisiana Creole Multi-Orthographic Text-to-Speech</h1>
    <p>This is an experimental extension of my orthographic converter.
    This tool uses <a href="https://github.com/dmort27/epitran" target="_blank">Epitran</a> and purpose built rulesets
    for grapheme-phoneme-grapheme conversions
    <br>After converting the orthography, another set of rules rewrites the input to be compatible with a target artificial speaker
    from Google Text-to-Speech.
     <br>Try it out on some <a href="https://archive.org/stream/ajs8769.0001.001.umich.edu/ajs8769.0001.001.umich.edu_djvu.txt" target="_blank">folktales</a>
    <br>
    <br>This is a very rough draft, more of a proof-of-concept than anything else. Since the synthesis engines are end to end, they (unpredictably)
    <br>spell things out instead of reading them sometimes.
    <br>This system ignores punctuation, but all other non-alphabetic symbols besides whitespace will cause errors.
    <br>Adjust the window size by clicking and dragging as needed.
</p>
    <h3>Spelling options (Most to least etymological)</h3>
        <p>
            <br><strong>French:</strong> Mainly used for historic texts through the 20th century.
            Note: This program converts spellings; it is not a French to Louisiana Creole translator.
            <br><strong>Kouri Vini:</strong> Standard developed by Christophe Landry
               <a href="http://www.mylhcv.com/guide-to-louisiana-creole-orthography/" target="_blank">
               (Guide to Louisiana Creole Orthography)</a>
               and used in the
               <a href="https://sites.google.com/view/learnlouisianacreole/home?authuser=0" target="_blank">
               Ti Liv Kréyòl</a>
            <br><strong>DLC: </strong> The system developed by Thomas A. Klingler and used
            in the <a href="https://frit.indiana.edu/research/publications/dictionary-of-la-creole.html" target="_blank">
                Dictionary of Louisiana Creole. </a>
            <br><strong>IPA: </strong>The International Phonetic Alphabet.
            Note: Since the focus is spelling, some distinctions made by linguists
            are collapsed here.
        </p>
